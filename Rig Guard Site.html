01The NPT crisis

Every hour the rig is
down costs $45K–$60K.

Sensor fleets generate signals every second. But signals don't fix rigs — engineers do. And by the time an engineer correlates the signal, calls the warehouse, and types the work order, the rig has been silent for two days. Roughly 70% of that response time is lost inside manual system lookups — parts availability, work history, and technician scheduling.

Predict → Context → Expose
→ Propose → Execute.

Five luminous nodes connect by a continuous particle stream — the loop runs once per anomaly and never asks a human for what it can answer alone.

Execute

05Two-mode autonomy

One loop. Three confidence gates.

Confidence below 85% is logged for review. Between 85% and 94% routes to a Maintenance Lead for one-tap confirmation. At 95% and above, Rig-Guard executes — with a 30-minute cancel window.

Watch · <85%
Observe & Log
Confidence below 85%

Signals are logged to SAC for trend analysis. No work order. No human page. The model keeps learning.

  • Logged to SAC
  • No WO drafted
  • Model retraining loop
HITL · 85–94%
Human-on-the-Loop
Confidence 85% to 94%

Maintenance Lead receives a Joule-style alert with a pre-drafted WO. One tap to confirm; one tap to defer. Median ack time < 60s.

  • Pre-drafted WO
  • Part + technician resolved
  • 1-tap confirm or defer
Autonomous · ≥95%
Fully Autonomous
Confidence 95% and above

SBPA executes the WO, reserves parts, and assigns a technician. The Lead is notified post-fact with a 30-minute cancel window.

  • WO created in S/4HANA
  • Parts reserved · WMS
  • 30-min cancel window
